Why everything here is free

Most of the software I build is small, local-first desktop software. No subscriptions. No required accounts. No ads. No monthly fees.

If an app doesn't cost me money to operate, I don't want it to cost you money to use.

That means you can download it, try it, and keep using it for as long as you'd like without ever paying.

The Idea

This storefront operates on a simple principle called Value for Value.

Instead of asking you to pay before you know whether an app is useful, I'd rather let the software speak for itself.

If it helps you accomplish your goals, saves you time, teaches you something, or simply makes your day a little easier, then it has created value.

If you'd like to return some value back, you can do so through the optional Support page.

If not, that's completely okay too.

Why this matters to me

I've gone through periods where money was tight.

Some of the most helpful tools I've ever used were created by generous people who made them freely available. Those projects helped me learn, build, and keep moving forward when I couldn't always afford paid software.

This is my way of paying that forward.

I want someone who's just starting out, between jobs, in school, building a side hustle, or simply trying to make ends meet to have access to useful software without wondering whether they can justify buying it.

If one of my apps helps you, then I've accomplished what I set out to do.

Returning Value

My hope is that some people will eventually say:

"I've been using this app for months. It's genuinely helped me. I'd like to support the person who built it."

That support helps fund new features, bug fixes, and future projects while keeping existing apps free for everyone else.

Whether that's $2, $10, or simply recommending an app to a friend, every bit of support helps.

A Note About AI

Some future projects may use AI services that charge per request. In those cases, any cost would simply cover the AI provider's usage fees, not access to the software itself.

Whenever possible, I will continue building local-first software that works entirely on your own computer.

Where Your Support Goes

I believe in being transparent about how support is used.

The first goal isn't to maximize profit—it's simply to build a better development workspace so I can create better software.

Current priorities include:

  • A modern development laptop that can comfortably handle larger software projects, game development, AI tools, and video editing.
  • A reliable keyboard and mouse for everyday development.
  • A docking station to simplify my workspace.
  • Replacement cables and other small desk essentials.
  • Software licenses or services that directly improve the quality of the apps I build.

Every contribution — whether it's a few dollars or simply sharing an app with someone who might find it useful—helps make the next project possible.

Thank You

Whether you support the projects financially, report a bug, suggest a feature, contribute code, or simply use something I've built, thank you.

I genuinely hope these little tools make your day a little easier. ❤️
