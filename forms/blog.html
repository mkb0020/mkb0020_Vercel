About Data XP

Data XP is a blog about learning how indie games actually succeed — through data, experimentation, and real-world testing.

I’m a solo indie developer exploring what works (and what doesn’t) in game development and marketing. Instead of guessing, I use publicly available data, market analysis, and hands-on experiments to understand how games grow, get discovered, and reach players.

I approach indie game development from a data-first, experimental perspective. Rather than relying on long-time gaming experience, I focus on analyzing real market trends and testing ideas through small experiments and personal projects.

Each post is part research, part experiment, and part personal dev log. I break down trends in games, test ideas in real projects, and share what happens when theory meets reality.

What you'll find here

  • Market analysis — trends, patterns, and what’s actually selling in indie games
  • Experiments — testing ideas in real projects and documenting results
  • Dev insights — lessons learned from building and iterating
  • Data breakdowns — turning messy information into clear takeaways

The idea

Think of this as earning XP:

  • analyzing the game market
  • testing ideas in real builds
  • learning from what works (and what fails)

This isn’t just theory — it’s a quest to turn data into experience, and experience into better games.

Side quests, experiments, and boss fights included.
